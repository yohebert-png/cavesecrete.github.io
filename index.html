How It Works

Simple, personal, and transparent.

Sourcing

  • Only French wine — Burgundy, Bordeaux, Rhône, Loire, Champagne and more.
  • Sourced through auctions, private collections and trusted importers.
  • Every bottle is something I would be comfortable putting in my own cellar.

Pricing

  • You pay exactly what I pay at auction or from suppliers.
  • A flat 10% sourcing fee covers time and research.
  • I compare prices in Australia and France before buying.
  • I only buy when the value is genuinely good.

Ordering

  1. Browse the current selection.
  2. Text me with the bottles and quantities you’d like.
  3. I confirm availability and reserve your bottles.
  4. Payment is made via bank transfer.
  5. Pickup or delivery is organised directly.

About Cave Secrète

I grew up in Burgundy, not far from Chablis, and I’ve always mostly drunk French wine. I keep a private cellar in Burgundy with more than 400 bottles, and over the last ten years I have purchased more than 300 bottles of French wine in Australia.

Since moving to Australia, I’ve seen how difficult it can be to find good French wine at a fair price. Bottle shop prices are often double or triple what you’d pay in France, and many importers tend to bring wines that match Australian tastes — more fruit-forward, riper, bigger styles — rather than classic French profiles.

When I first arrived, I didn’t know anything about Australian wine (I still don’t), so I would go to a bottle shop, pick a random bottle, and often end up disappointed. I realised this is probably what happens to Australians trying to pick a French bottle for the first time: confusing labels, inconsistent quality, and often overpriced.

Cave Secrète is my way of making French wine a little easier for a small group of families and friends. The principles are simple:

  • Only French wine.
  • Only bottles I would buy for myself.
  • Only when the price is reasonable compared with France.
  • Only cost + 10% sourcing fee. I don’t make money from this — if someone buys ten bottles, I buy myself a nice bottle as my reward.
  • Only for a small, private group — I can’t source large quantities.

Everyone’s taste is different. The goal isn’t to tell people what they should like, but to give them the chance to explore good French wine without the usual bottle-shop mark-ups.
